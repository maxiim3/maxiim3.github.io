# Top Developer Websites — Trend Analysis Report

**Date:** 2026-02-15
**Scope:** 25 top frontend/JS developers personal websites
**Purpose:** Input reference for CV/Portfolio PRD creation

---

## Executive Summary

The personal website landscape among top developers reveals a **polarized ecosystem**: minimalist identity hubs vs. full content engines, with almost nothing in between. The traditional portfolio site is dead. What replaces it depends on the developer's economic model: open-source maker or educator-entrepreneur.

**Key opportunity for Max's site:** A site with strong visual identity and deliberate positioning would be immediately distinctive in this space, where most top devs default to bare-minimum pages.

---

## Major Trends

### 1. The Death of the Portfolio Site

The personal site as a "showcase" is gone. The overwhelming majority have a near-empty page — a name, a tagline, some links. The site is a **hub**, not a destination. Legitimacy comes from GitHub, shipped code, talks, and community reputation.

**Implication for Max:** Don't mimic this pattern. These devs can afford minimalism because they have 50k+ GitHub stars. A career-pivoting developer needs the site to *do the selling work itself*.

### 2. Two Economic Models, No Middle Ground

| Model | Representatives | Site Pattern |
|-------|----------------|--------------|
| **Makers** (open-source) | Evan You, Rich Harris, Shu Ding, Seb Chopin | Minimal site, GitHub-centric, sponsoring/employment |
| **Educators** (content) | Kent C. Dodds, Wes Bos, Addy Osmani, Kyle Simpson | Full content engine: courses, podcasts, books, funnels |

Almost nobody occupies the space between these two poles.

**Implication for Max:** Position closer to the "maker with strong communication" archetype — demonstrate deep Vue/Nuxt expertise with a site that itself *is* the proof of competence.

### 3. GitHub as the Real CV

For framework creators, the GitHub profile IS the business card. Stars are the credibility metric (Bun: 87k, Svelte: 86k, webpack: 66k, Astro: 57k). README.md is more polished than most personal sites.

**Implication for Max:** GitHub profile should be polished and linked prominently, but the site must compensate for lower star counts with quality of presentation and narrative.

### 4. The Minimalist Aesthetic Dominates

Two clear camps:
- **Minimalist/Hacker**: Jake Archibald, Dan Abramov, Jeremy Keith — flat design, raw text, zero visual flair. Signal: "my content is enough."
- **Corporate polish**: Guillermo Rauch, Addy Osmani — clean but intentional design with metrics, logos, strategic layout.

Bold visual design is **almost entirely absent**. This is the biggest gap.

**Implication for Max:** A visually distinctive site (without being gratuitous) would stand out massively. The bar for "impressive design" in this space is very low.

### 5. Identity-Driven Domains

Strong preference for creative TLDs: `.dev`, `.me`, `.gg`, `.mov`, `.in`. Traditional `.com` feels dated. `shud.in` plays on name, `danabra.mov` is the Bluesky handle.

**Implication for Max:** Consider a distinctive domain that reinforces personal brand.

### 6. The Humanizing Detail

Nearly every site includes one deliberate non-technical element — karaoke, BBQ, cheese, basketball. Pattern: establish technical credibility first, then signal approachability. The personal brand rests on personality, not just competence ("Human-centric development").

**Implication for Max:** Include a curated personal touch — the 10-year non-tech background could be the differentiator, not a liability.

### 7. Career Pivot as Narrative

Several sites document professional transitions: Evan You's VoidZero pivot, Fred K. Schott's Snowpack-to-Astro, Tobias Koppers' webpack-to-Vercel. Personal sites function as **changelogs for professional evolution**.

**Implication for Max:** The non-traditional path INTO tech is a powerful narrative. Frame it as a strength, not a gap.

### 8. The AI Repositioning Wave

Brutal and fast. Addy Osmani pivoted from Chrome/Web Vitals to AI with 4-5 books on vibe coding in months. Kent C. Dodds launched EpicAI. Those not pivoting toward AI content are going silent or staying niche.

**Implication for Max:** Consider how AI/Vue intersection could be featured, but don't chase the trend — own the Vue/Nuxt specialization.

### 9. Long-Form Blog in Decline

Content production migrating to YouTube and social media. Active blogs are either content machines (Addy) or deep thought pieces (Dan Abramov, Lea Verou, Jake Archibald). Many top devs haven't published since 2023.

**Implication for Max:** A blog is optional. If included, quality over quantity. The site's primary job is conversion (interviews, freelance), not content marketing.

### 10. What's Missing Everywhere

- No visual portfolios
- No bold/distinctive design
- No decentralized identity presence
- No traditional resumes or skill matrices
- No interactive demonstrations of expertise

These gaps are all opportunities.

## Architectural Patterns Summary

| Pattern | Frequency | Examples |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Single-page hub (name + links) | ~40% | Evan You, Shu Ding, Sebastian McKenzie |
| Blog-centric | ~30% | Dan Abramov, Jake Archibald, Jeremy Keith |
| Content/course platform | ~20% | Kent C. Dodds, Wes Bos, Addy Osmani |
| GitHub-only presence | ~10% | Rich Harris, Ryan Carniato, Misko Hevery |

## Technology Patterns

- Next.js/Vercel ecosystem dominant among those with actual sites
- Static generation preferred
- Minimal JavaScript on personal pages
- Dark mode increasingly standard
- Performance-first approach (these devs practice what they preach)

## Strategic Direction for Max's CV Site

### Chosen approach: Minimalist Hub
Follow the dominant pattern among top devs (Theo Browne / t3.gg as primary reference). Simple, clean, shippable in one session.

### Principles
1. **Minimalist by choice** — Clean single-page or few-page hub
2. **Content over flash** — Let experience and projects speak
3. **Ship fast** — Online tonight, iterate later
4. **Nuxt 3 + static** — The tech choice itself signals competence without needing to show off
